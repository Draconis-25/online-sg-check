The Galactic Senate is the legislature assembly and the main decision making body of the fictional Star Wars Galactic Republic. Presided by the Supreme Chancellor, the highest rank in the Galactic Republic, the senate aimed at mediating disputes alongside creating laws and regulations. Though the Galactic Republic and thus the senate had no military power aside from guards in the beginning, through the last days of republic, the senate has worked with the clone army, and the Jedi Council.

Galactic Senate Rules of Procedure

ALIASES

  1. For this document, the following aliases shall be used
    • GR: Galactic Republic
    • GRM: Galactic Republic member, including member
      1. Planets,
      2. Moons,
      3. Star systems,
      4. Non-governmental Organizations,
      5. Astronomical objects
    • GSL: General Speakers List

PART I. MEETINGS

  1. Meetings of the Galactic Senate will be held at a time designated by the Office of the Chancellor.

  2. Meetings of the Galactic Senate will be held in the Galactic Senate Building, unless else is specified.

PART II. AGENDA

  1. The preliminary agenda for the meeting is set by the Office of the Chancellor prior to the first session and the agenda is (TBA) Clone Wars.

PART III. REPRESENTATION

  1. Each GRM shall be represented by one senator, and if needed, representatives, whose number shall be specified by the legislative assembly of the subject GRM.

PART IV: OFFICERS

Supreme Chancellor

  1. The Supreme Chancellor of the GR, shall open the first meeting of the assembly.

  2. In the absence of the Supreme Chancellor, the Vice Chair of the Senate shall serve as the temporary Supreme Chancellor.

  3. The rapporteur(s) of the committee shall be selected by the Office of the Chancellor, prior to the assembly, and shall be announced by the Supreme Chancellor, at the opening ceremony of the assembly.

  4. In addition to exercising, the powers conferred upon him elsewhere by these rules and the powers of the vice chair, the Supreme Chancellor shall preside at the meetings of the Galactic Senate, propose the adjournment and/or the suspension of the debate. direct the discussion, directly pass motions, join in debates, put motions to vote, propose the closure of the list of speakers, decide on the times allocated for the GSLs and the opening speeches, and alter the durations of motions.

  5. If the Supreme Chancellor finds it necessary to be absent from a meeting or any part thereof, he shall designate the Vice Chair to take his place.

  6. If a representative shows inappopriate behaviour, the Supreme Chancellor holds the power to take the representative out ofthe meeting.

Vice Chair

  1. In addition to exercising, the powers conferred upon him elsewhere by these rules, the Vice Chair shall ensure the observance of rules, declare the opening and closing of each session, accord the right to speak, promote general agreement, act as the spokesperson of the Supreme Chancellor, announce decisions, and inform the committee of the current status of the events.

Rapporteur

  1. The rapporteur of the committee shall:
    1. Make notes of everything spoken within the context of the meeting,
    2. Receive and distribute the documents of the committee,
    3. In the absence of the Vice Chair, take his place, if needed.

Voting Status of the Executive Officers

  1. The Supreme Chancellor, Vice Chair, Vice Chair acting as the chancellor, and the Rapporteur shall not vote in the committee.

PART V: CONDUCT OF BUSINESS

Quorum

  1. The Supreme Chancellor may permit the debate to proceed when the senator and/or representatives of at least one third of the GRMs participating in the work of the assembly present. This number of present members shall be required for any decision to be taken.

Speeches

  1. No one may address the Galactic Senate without having previously obtained the permission by the executive officers. The executive officers shall be in charge of drawing up a list of speakers.

  2. The Supreme Chancellor and/or the Vice Chair may call a speaker to order if their remarks are not relevant to the subject under discussion.

Points of Order

  1. During the discussion of any matter, a participating representative may at any time raise a point of order, which shall be decided immediately by the Supreme Chancellor in accordance with these rules.

  2. A representative may not, in raising a point of order, speak on the substance of the matter under discussion.

Right of Reply

  1. The Vice Chair shall accord the right of reply to a representative of any GRM participating who may request it, if it's seen useful to the flow of debate by the executive officers.

  2. A right of reply may not be arised for a right of reply speech.

  3. A right of reply speech shall be at most 45 seconds long, and be done following the speech in which the right of reply was raised.

Closure of Debate

  1. A representative may at any time move to the closure of the debate. In addition to the proposer of the motion, permission to speak on the motion shall be accorded only to one representative in favour and to one opposing the adjournment, after which the motion shall, subject to the clause 13 of this section, be immediately put to the vote.

  2. This motion may be overruled by the Supreme Chancellor.

Suspension or Ajournment of the Meeting

  1. A representative may at any time move to the suspension or adjournment of the meeting. No discussions on these motions shall be permitted, and they shall, subject to the clause 13 of this section, be immediately put to the vote.

  2. These motions may be overruled by the Supreme Chancellor.

Order of Motions

  1. The motions below shall have predence in the following order over all other motions before the meeting:
    1. To suspend the meeting;
    2. To adjourn the meeting;
    3. To close the debate.

Proposals

  1. Proposals shall be submitted to the Rapporteur, who shall circulate the copies to all delegates. As a general rule, no proposal shall be discussed or put to the vote at any meeting unless copies of it has been sent to the present GRM representatives.

Withdrawal of Proposals and Motions

  1. A proposal or motion may be withdrawn by it's proposer at any time before voting on it has started. A withdrawn proposal or motion may be reintroduced by any representative.

Invitations to the Jedi Council

  1. A motion can be raised by the committee to invite, at most one Jedi, when the intervention of the Jedi Council is seen necessary.

  2. This motion may be overruled by the Supreme Chancellor.

Reconsideration of Rejected Motions

  1. If the Committee decides on the passing of thereof motion, with two-thirds majority of the representatives present in a roll-call vote, it shall be reintroduced to the committe, within the permission of the Supreme Chancellor.

Points of Personal Privilage

  1. If any representative has personal needs, they can use "personal privilage" point to inform and get permission from the executive officers. This type of point can be used with note papers.

  2. Point of personal privilage due to inaudibilty may be arised at any time except for the voting procedure.

Points of Parliamentary Inquiry

  1. This point may be utilized in order to clarify questions surrounding this document.

Points of Information

  1. Point of information may be used to get the context of motions, or speeches, if not understood by the representative raising this point.

PART VI: DECISION MAKING

General Agreement

  1. The Committee shall make its best endeavours to ensure that a general agreement is achieved on the work of the committee.

Voting Rights

  1. Each representative partcipating shall have one vote.

Meaning of the Phrase "Representative Present and Voting"

  1. "Present and voting" means that the representative is present and will be casting an affirmitive or negative vote. Present and voting representatives shall not abstain.

Method of Voting

  1. The committee shall normally vote by show of hands, except that a representative may request a roll-call voting, which shall then be taken in the English alphabetical order of the names of the participants. The name of each present state shall be called, and the representative shall reply "yes", "no" or "abstension". The representative may request an explanation of vote speech by stating "with rights".

Conduct During Voting

  1. After the Supreme Chancellor starts the voting procedure, no representative shall interrupt the voting except for a point of order concerning the process of voting.

Explanation of Vote

  1. Representatives who request as specified by the clause 4 of this section may make a 45 second speech, only aboout the reason behind their votes.

Order of Voting on Proposals

  1. If two or more proposals, relate to the same question, unless the Committe decides otherwise, be voted on in the order they were submitted. If deemed necessary, the Committee may decide to vote on the next proposal.

  2. Revised proposals shall be voted on in the order in which they were first submitted, unless the revision is ssubstentially different from the original. In that case the original shall be considered withdrawn.

PART VII: FLOW OF THE COMMITTEE

Opening Ceremony

  1. The committee shall start with the anthem of the GR, and the speech of the Supreme Chancellor, where the following shall be announced:
    1. The assigned rapporteur,
    2. The agenda,
    3. The current state of the Clone Wars and other related events.

Role Call

  1. After the opening ceremony the Committee shall continue with the role call, in which the executive officers shall call the representatives' names, in the English alphabetical order. When announced, the representative shall declare him/herself as "present" or, "present and voting". Any representative not replying shall be noted as absent.

Opening Speeches and the Opening of the Debate

  1. After the role call, opening speeches from each representative shall be taken for 90 seconds. This duration can be altered by the Supreme Chancellor.

  2. Immediately after the last opening speech, opening of the debate shall be voted on. With its passage, the floor shall be declared open for any points or motions. If failed, the vote shall be repeated until passing.

  3. Opening speeches of the representatives who were late and came after the opening of the debate, shall not be entertained.

PART VIII: SUBSIDIARY Organizations

  1. This committee may establish such subsidiary organs as it deems necessary, which shall get their officers elected by this committee.

PART IX: LANGUAGES AND RECORDS

Language of the Committee

  1. English shall be the official language of the committee.

Interpretation

  1. A representative may speak in a language other than a language of the Committee, if the delegation concerned provides for interpretation into one such language.

Languages of Official Documents

  1. Official documents shall be written in the official language of the Committee.

  2. A draft resolution shall be translated to aurabesh and be circulated within the committee after it has been passed.
