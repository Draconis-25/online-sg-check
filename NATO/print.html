NATO Rules of Procedure

PART I. MEETINGS

  1. Meetings of the North Atlantic Treaty Organization will be held at a time and place designated by the sponsoring institutions.

  2. Official language is English for the committee.

PART II. AGENDA

  1. The preliminary agenda for regular meetings of the Council and Committees shall be drawn up by the sponsoring institutions and communicated to the members prior to the opening of the first official session and the agenda is supporting the African Union

PART III. REPRESENTATION

  1. Each NATO country and partner country shall be represented by one delegate.

PART IV: THE CHAIRBOARD

CHAIRBOARD

  1. The Committee will have a Chairperson from the Secretariat who will have full powers to ensure proper functioning of the committee.

  2. The Chairperson may preside over all meetings, or may designate its co-chair to do so.

  3. The Chairboard shall have the responsibility of ensuring the smooth operation of the Committee through interpretation and enforcement of the Rules. In addition to exercising powers described elsewhere in the Rules, the Chairperson shall declare the opening and closing of each meeting, direct discussions, accord the right to speak and announce decisions.He/she shall rule on points of order and, subject to these Rules, shall have complete control of the proceedings at any meeting.

  4. The decision of the Chair may be appealed by any delegate. This motion is debatable by one delegate in favor and one against, after which the motion shall be put to a vote. The Chairperson’s decision will stand unless overruled by a two-thirds majority of members present and voting.

Rapporteur

  1. The rapporteur of the committee shall:
    1. Make notes of everything spoken within the context of the meeting,
    2. Receive and distribute the documents of the committee,
    3. In the absence of the Vice Chair, take his place, if needed.

Academic Asisstant

  1. The academic asisstant may help the delegates by:
    1. Giving information
    2. Suggesting resolutions if it does not interferes the debate.

PART V: CONDUCT OF BUSINNESS

Quorum

  1. The chairboard may permit the debate to proceed when at least one third of the delegates participating in the work of the assembly present. This number of present members shall be required for any decision to be taken.

Speeches

  1. No one may address the without having previously obtained the permission by the chairboard. The chairboard shall be in charge of drawing up a list of speakers.

Points of Order

  1. During the discussion of any matter, a participating delegate may at any time raise a point of order, which shall be decided immediately by the chairboard in accordance with these rules.

  2. A delegate may not, in raising a point of order, speak on the substance of the matter under discussion.

RIGHT of REPLY

  1. The chairboard shall accord the right of reply to a delegate participating who may request it, if it's seen useful to the flow of debate by the executive officers.
  2. A right of reply may not be arised for a right of reply speech.
  3. A right of reply speech shall be at most 45 seconds long, and be done following the speech in which the right of reply was raised.

Closure of the Debate

  1. A delegate may at any time move to the closure of the debate. In addition to the proposer of the motion, permission to speak on the motion shall be accorded only to one representative in favour and to one opposing the adjournment, after which the motion shall, subject to the order of motions, be immediately put to the vote.

  2. This motion may be overruled by the chairboard.

Suspension or Adjournment of the Meeting

  1. A delegate may at any time move to the suspension or adjournment of the meeting. No discussions on these motions shall be permitted, and they shall,subject to the order of motions, be immediately put to the vote.

  2. These motions may be overruled by the chairboard.

Order of Motions

  1. The motions below shall have predence in the following order over all other motions before the meeting:

    1. To suspend the meeting;
    2. To adjourn the meeting;
    3. To close the debate.

PART VI: DECISION MAKING

General Agreement

  1. The Committee shall make its best endeavours to ensure that a general agreement is achieved on the work of the committee.

Voting Rights

  1. Each representative partcipating shall have one vote.

Meaning of the Phrase "Present and Voting"

  1. "Present and voting" means that the delegate is present and will be casting an affirmitive or negative vote. Present and voting representatives shall not abstain.

Method of Voting

  1. The committee shall normally vote by show of hands, except that a representative may request a roll-call voting, which shall then be taken in the English alphabetical order of the names of the participants. The name of each present state shall be called, and the representative shall reply "yes", "no" or "abstension". The representative may request an explanation of vote speech by stating "with rights".

Conduct During Voting

  1. After the chairboard starts the voting procedure, no representative shall interrupt the voting except for a point of order concerning the process of voting.

Explanation of Vote

  1. Representatives who request as specified by the clause 4 of this section may make a 45 second speech, only aboout the reason behind their votes.

Order of Voting on Proposals

  1. If two or more proposals, relate to the same question, unless the Committe decides otherwise, be voted on in the order they were submitted. If deemed necessary, the Committee may decide to vote on the next proposal.

  2. Revised proposals shall be voted on in the order in which they were first submitted, unless the revision is ssubstentially different from the original. In that case the original shall be considered withdrawn.

PART VI: FLOW OF THE COMMITTEE

SETTING THE AGENDA

  1. The delegates are expected to raise a motion to set the agenda.
  2. This motion requires a second. If there is no opposition to the motion,it is adopted by the chairboard.If there is opposition,the chairboard may accept 2-4 opposition speaches and move on with the voting procedure.

Roll Call

  1. At the beginning of each session, the chairboard shall record the status of the Members present and determine the required majorities.
  2. The roll call shall be performed in the English alphabetical order.
  3. Members shall state their status as present or present and voting when they are called upon.
  4. Members that have not replied to the roll call will be treated as absentees, even if they are physically present, until they send a message paper to the chairboard stating their presence.
  5. Members that have missed more than half of the time allocated for the session cannot alter their statuses from ‘absent’. Such Members cannot join the debate, nor exercise their voting rights. Members marked absent may not join the debate or vote until their status is changed by the chairboard.

OPENING SPEECHES

  1. Every delegate should deliver a opening speech after the agenda is set in the first official session.
  2. The opening speeches cannot exceed 1.30 minutes.

GENERAL SPEAKERS LIST

  1. A general speakers list will be formed by the chairboard at the beginning of every offical session except for the first session.
  2. Delegates who are wishing to speak should raise their placards prior to the chairboards call.
  3. The gsl speeches cannot exceed 1 minute.
  4. Delegates should come to the lectern after having the floor to give their speeches.
  5. Delegates wishing to remove their names from general speakers list should send a message paper stating their will to the chairboard.
  6. The chairboard preserves every right to close the general speakers list due to flow of the committee.

POINTS

1.POINT OF PERSONAL PRIVILEGE

Whenever a delegate expriences personal discomfort,he or she may raise this point to request that the discomfort be corrected.This point can only intrupt a speaker if the delegate speaking is inaudible.

2.POINT OF ORDER

If there is any concerns upon the intruption of the rules of procedure in the committee this point may be raised.This point may never intrupt a speaker.

3.POINT OF PARLIAMENTARY INQUIRY

When the floor is open,this point may be raised in order to ask a question regarding the rules of procedure.This point may never intrupt a speaker.

4.POINT OF INFORMATION

Any kinds of questions that cannot be obtained by the other points may be asked as a point of information when the floor is open.This may never intrupt a speaker.

MOTIONS

  1. Delegates should give their motions prior to chairboards call for the opening of the floor.

  2. After the floor is open points have the top priority in the order.

  3. Delegates should give their motion after gaining chairboards recognition by raising their placards.

  4. If there are more than one motions,the motion stated with a longer time range than the other motions will be voted first.

  5. A motion requires a second and no objections to pass.If there is an objection the chairboard will have a procedural voting.

  6. The chairboard preserves every right to grant motions without going for voting and limit the time for the motions.

  7. A motion sentence may be structured like this "Motion to talk about ___ in moderated/unmoderated caucus for ___ minutes,individual speaker time being ___ minutes.".

    MOTION TO ADJOURN THE MEETING

    This motin may be raised to suspend all the activities of the body until ILERIMUN24 and requires a two-thirds majority to pass.

    MOTION TO SUSPEND THE MEETING

    This motion may be raised to Suspend the Meeting in order to suspend all functions of the body until the next meeting.

    MOTION TO CLOSE THE DEBATE

    This motion may be raised to close the general debate or the debate on amendments.

    MOTION TO EXTEND THE CAUCUS

    This motion may only be raised during a caucus to extend its total duration.

    MOTION TO TERMINATE THE CAUCUS

    Is in order during a caucus unless there is already an entertained speaker for the related motion at that particular moment.

CAUCUSES

  1. The time given for a caucus cannot exceed 20 minutes.
  2. Caucuses cannot be given a extention that is longer than the original caucus's duration
  3. Delegates cannot give extention to extention.
  4. Caucuses may be terminated prior to chairboards consent.
  5. First caucus of each session should be a moderated caucus.
  6. The individual speaker time should be stated for a moderated caucus.
  7. There are two types of caucuses:

MODERATED CAUCUS

  1. Delegates are not permitted to 
    a.Talk without the chairboards permission
    b.Walk around 
    c.Talk to other delegates.
  2. Delegates may communicate by message papers.

UNMODERATED CAUCUS

  1. Delegates may
    a. Talk without chairboards permission
    b. Walk around
    c. Talk to other delegates.

SEMI MODERATED CAUCUS

  1. Delegates may talk freely without intrupting each others speeches
  2. Delegates are not permitted to walk around

Dress Code

All attendees must:

  • Refrain from using accessories that may promote any groups that are unrelated to their roles and are not said to be allowed in this document
  • Refer to the conservative and formal side, when in doubt

Male attendees:

  • Should always wear three-piece(jacket, pants, dress shirt) suits.
  • Accessories:
    • Mandatory:
      • Neck/Bow tie
    • Allowed:
      • Rings
      • Belts
      • Lapel/Tie pins (as long as it doesn't violate the first rule)
      • Suspenders
      • Cufflinks/Earrings/clips (as long as it doesn't violate the first rule)
      • Pocket Squares
      • Watches
    • Refrain from:
      • Bracelets
      • Hats
      • Gloves
      • Necklaces
      • Graphic ties
      • Chains
  • Shirts:
    • Refrain from:
      • Graphic shirts
      • Flamboyant colors
    • Allowed collars:
      • Classic
      • Semi-Spread
      • Spread
      • Button Down
      • Wings
      • Small Wings
    • Unallowed Collars
      • Eyelet
      • Mandarin
      • Bands
      • Standing
  • Jacket & Pants:
    • Always wear matching suits
    • Italian style is recommended
    • Prefer darker colors
    • Refrain from informal patterns
  • Always use formal shoes

Female atendees:

  • Should wear
    • Pants/skirts/dresses
      • The minimum skirt length is two fingers lower than knee length
  • Should refrain from
    • informal patterns
    • flamboyant colors
  • Shirts:
    • Refrain from:
      • Graphic Shirts
      • Flamboyant colors
      • Revealing styles
        • Maximum reveal can be three fingers under the neckline
  • Always use formal shoes
  • Are encouraged to wear
    • Jackets
  • There are no required accessories for female delegates however it is allowed to wear these:
    • Jewelry (as long as it doesn't violate the first rule)
    • Foulard (refrain from flamboyant colors and informal patterns)
    • Hair accessories (crowns are only permitted for the delegates and chair board in the crisis committees)
    • Watches
    • Pins(as long as it doesn't violate the first rule)
